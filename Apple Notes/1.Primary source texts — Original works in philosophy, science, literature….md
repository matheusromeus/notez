1.	**Primary source texts** — Original works in philosophy, science, literature, law, and religion. No intermediaries. Access raw thought.
	2.	**Master-level lectures** — University-level material from elite institutions or domain experts. Compressed transmission of distilled expertise.
	3.	**Technical manuals and specs** — Engineering docs, RFCs, standards, whitepapers. Precision over pedagogy.
	4.	**Well-edited books with long half-lives** — Time-tested works in any domain. Filtered by cultural survival.
	5.	**Historical records** — Chronicles, biographies, timelines, economic data. Contextualize present within pattern.
	6.	**Art criticism and architecture theory** — For training taste, form analysis, and design perception.
	7.	**Documentaries with primary footage** — Visual history with minimal commentary. Observe reality in situ.
	8.	**High-level debates or interviews** — Between deeply competent adversaries. Reveals limits of knowledge.
	9.	**Mathematical proofs and algorithm derivations** — Structure cognition. Model abstraction.
	10.	**Codebases of canonical software** — UNIX, Git, CPython, Redis. Learn from architecture, not tutorials.
	11.	**Legal arguments and judicial opinions** — For logic structuring, edge-case framing, and systemic reasoning.
	12.	**Poetry and classical music** — Cognitive pattern compression. Tune rhythm, metaphor, and emotion detection.
	13.	**Ethnographies and anthropology texts** — Expand conceptual bandwidth beyond cultural default.
	14.	**Military strategy and game theory** — Teach long-horizon reasoning under constraints and deception.
	15.	**Studio notes from masters** — Da Vinci, Miyazaki, Kubrick. Observe internal process.

Reject content optimized for engagement, reaction, or trend. Favor signal over frequency. Depth over novelty. Time density over consumption rate.
